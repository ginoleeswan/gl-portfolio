## The constraint you can't skip
Karma Kart runs from one Expo codebase on iOS, Android, and the web. Adding a paid tier ran
straight into a rule that has nothing to do with my code: Apple and Google require their own
in-app-purchase systems for digital goods sold inside native apps. You can't drop a Stripe
checkout into an App Store build. The web has no such rule and no 30% cut, so an ordinary payment
processor is fine there.

So "add subscriptions" was really two jobs that happened to share a UI.

## Why one tool didn't cover both
I looked at using a single billing SDK across all three platforms and decided against it.

RevenueCat is good on native and doesn't do web at all. It wraps StoreKit and Google Play
Billing and handles receipt validation, entitlements, and restore-purchases — the parts of native
IAP I didn't want to write by hand. But it isn't a web checkout, so using it on the web would
mean rebuilding the part it already does for me.

Going the other way doesn't work: routing native purchases through a web processor like Paddle or
Stripe is what gets an App Store build rejected.

## What I built
There's one paid tier, Karma Kart+, and two ways to pay for it.

On iOS and Android it goes through RevenueCat over StoreKit and Play Billing, which holds the
entitlement state on the device. On the web it goes through Paddle. I picked Paddle for the web
because it acts as the merchant of record and deals with sales tax and VAT, so I don't have to
register for tax in every country a web subscriber might be in.

Above that, the app doesn't care which processor was used. It checks one thing — is this user
premium? — through a single entitlement check in `lib/payments`, and the platform difference
stays behind that check.

## The cost
Two payment paths mean two records of "is this person subscribed," reconciled into one
entitlement. That's more to maintain than a single Stripe integration would be on a web-only
product. I took it on because I wanted the app installable on the App Store and sellable on the
open web from the same codebase, and for a shopping app meant to be wherever people shop, the
extra wiring was worth it.
