## The problem
Plenty of people want to shop their values, but at the shelf there's no data and no time —
ethics research doesn't fit in the three seconds before something goes in the cart. Karma Kart
puts a verdict in those three seconds.

## What I built
A barcode scanner that resolves a product to its parent company and returns a personalized
**Karma Score** (0–100) across eight values, on a single **Expo SDK 55** codebase for iOS,
Android, and Web. Scans hit a Supabase Edge Function that enriches data with **Gemini 2.5
Flash**; product lookups fan out across Open Food Facts in a five-source waterfall so one
source's missing field isn't a dead end.

## Decisions that mattered
**The score is personal, and the math is testable.** An onboarding "this or that" quiz sets
per-value weights; the score normalizes those weights and sums them against company pillar
scores. I kept that algorithm in a pure `lib/` module with no React Native imports, so it runs
under Jest in milliseconds and the grading is provable, not vibes.

**Payments split by platform on purpose.** Native iOS/Android monetize through **RevenueCat**
to satisfy Apple/Google in-app-purchase rules, while the web tier runs on **Paddle**. One
subscription concept, two payment rails — chosen to match each platform's constraints instead
of fighting them.

**The AI key is server-side only.** Gemini runs inside the Edge Function with the key stored as
a Supabase secret, never in the app bundle.

## What was hard
Trusting third-party product data. Barcode databases are full of gaps and conflicts, so I built
the lookup as a ranked waterfall across five sources and let Gemini handle *enrichment* rather
than be the source of truth — the score still resolves when the data is thin.

## Outcome
A store-ready app on iOS, Android, and Web with real monetization (RevenueCat + Paddle),
product analytics (PostHog), crash reporting (Sentry), and Maestro end-to-end flows — a
complete, shippable product, not a prototype.
