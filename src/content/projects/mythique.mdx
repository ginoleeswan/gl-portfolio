## The problem
Superhero lore lives scattered across wikis, none of them pleasant on a phone. I wanted
one place — fast, native-feeling, and identical on iOS, Android, and the web — to browse
thousands of characters across publishers without it feeling like you're reading a database.

## What I built
A cross-platform encyclopedia on a single **Expo / expo-router 4** codebase, pulling from
**SuperheroAPI** and **ComicVine** behind a Supabase layer. Characters render with Reanimated 4
transitions and a squircle-based card system, with TanStack Query handling fetching and caching.

## Decisions that mattered
**One codebase, three platforms.** I committed to Expo's managed workflow so the same
file-based routes ship to iOS, Android, and Web instead of maintaining a separate web build.
The cost is living inside Expo's native-module boundary; the payoff is one surface to design,
test, and fix — which, solo, is the difference between shipping and not.

**Supabase in front of the APIs, not just for auth.** Rather than hit two rate-limited comic
APIs on every view, I front them with Supabase so reads stay fast and the third-party keys
never reach the client.

## What was hard
Two external APIs with different shapes, gaps, and rate limits had to look like one clean
dataset. I normalized them behind the query layer so the UI never knows which source a
character came from, and missing fields degrade gracefully instead of breaking the screen.

## Outcome
One Expo codebase shipping to iOS, Android, and Web, searching **3,000+ characters** — and the
open-source repo has earned **58 stars** from other developers.
